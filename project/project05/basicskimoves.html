Skiing is a dangerous sport. No matter how experienced you are knowing the basics is incredibly crucial to order prevent injury to the best of your ability. Two moves you should know before getting on the lift is “pizza” and “french fries”.

“pizza”- A “pizza” is easily the simplest way to stop, whether you’re going down a green, blue, black, double black mountain, or your simply in line for the lift this move can help you out when you’re in a pinch and need to stop desperately. To execute this move, navigate your skis so that the tips of them are touching and the back end of your skis are far a part, creating a pizza like shape if you look at it from a birds eye view.

“French fries” – To execute this move navigate your skis into parallel lines, so that they look like two French fries next to one another. This move will allow you so smoothly glide against the snow and propel yourself forward.

For your first couple runs down the groomers it is in your best interest to make sure you execute either pizza and French fries as you create large “S” shapes all the way down. To maintain control over your run you need to make sure that your turns are not short and that you are traveling from one side of the mountain to the other slowly. Remember, your first season should be a time to focus on you technique and getting comfortable with managing to get all the way down the mountain during your runs without falling over. Although, sometimes falling is unavoidable so we’ll be discussing what to do in instances such as those on the next page!
